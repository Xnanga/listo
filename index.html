Info

Thank you for using Listo, the kanban-style tasklist manager for the solo worker! Below are some Q&A pointers on how to use Listo:


Q: How do I mark my task as super-important and/or high priority?

A: See the faint white bar on the left side of each task card? Click that to set a high, medium, or low priority.

Q: What's the big stupid bar at the top of the page?

A: My friend, that's the task timeline bar. When you set a due date on a task card, it'll appear up there to show you all the due tasks in order of what is due first.

Q: What's with the blue and purple colours in the task timeline bar?

A: Those let you know, at a glance, whether the due task is blocked or not. Purple = blocked. Blue = not blocked. Simples.

Q: I need more info on one of my due tasks showing in the task timeline bar. Wut do?

A: No problemo. Hover over the task you need more info on for like a second; an info bubble should appear giving you all the juicy task-related info you're looking for.

Q: But how do I even set a due date? This is all so confusing.

A: It's surprisingly simple and straightforward. Click the 3 dots at the top-right of a task card, select "Set Due Date", then choose a date in the calendar.

Q: I've completed my task - how do I mark it so I know it's done?

A: Whenever you want to change the status of a task card, just click the 3 dots at the top-right of the task card. You can mark it as complete in that masterfully-designed dropdown menu.

Q: I accidentally set the wrong task as complete, woe is me!

A: Fear not! Remember how you set the task to complete? Do the same thing again, you'll notice the button has magically changed to "Mark as Incomplete".

Q: Some inconsiderate bugger is holding me back from completing a task - how can I mark the task as Blocked?

A: What an outrage. Check the 3 dots dropdown menu on the task card and mark the task as blocked there. You can set it as no longer Blocked the exact same way.

Q: I have decided this task no longer deserves to live. How do I delete it?

A: You monster! If I was a task murderer, like you, I would click the 3 dots dropdown menu and hit the Delete button.

Q: I've created all these tasks and lists, I'm just going to lose them when I leave or refresh the page, right?

A: Not so! Listo automatically saves all your tasks, lists, due dates, and statuses. Unless you clear your cache, it'll all be here waiting for you next time you come on by. I got you homie.

Q: I want to change some settings - some stuff doesn't suit my style.

A: That button with the cog symbol at the top-right of the page, in the nav bar, is exactly what you're looking for.

Q: I want to burn it all to the ground by clearing all my tasks, lists, and everything else!

A: Easy there, are you sure? Okay, see the cross button at the top-right, in the navigation bar? Click that to clear absolutely everything.
